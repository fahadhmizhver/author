Emma Castellano never had much. A mother who left. A house that wasn't really hers. And a man who promised to keep her safe.

Thomas Wren was kind. He remembered her birthday, brought her books, made her feel seen in a way no one else did. He was the only adult she trusted—until the day he took her.

Now Emma wakes up in a cold, windowless room somewhere underground. There's a cage in the middle, a bucket in the corner, and another girl who's been trapped there longer than she can imagine.

Outside, everyone believes Thomas's story. He's the concerned family friend, helping with the search for the missing girl. But inside the walls, Emma begins to understand what he really is, and what happened to the others who came before her.

The Girl in the Walls is not about heroes or miracles. It's about what happens when the only person who ever showed you kindness turns out to be the monster waiting in the dark.

Khambrath Group is a private publishing company founded by MD. Fahadh MizhVer (Mohammed Fahadh Khambrath). Established to operate independently from mainstream distributors, the company focuses on developing, managing, and publishing literary works that explore human behavior, psychology, and modern authority.

The group's first major release, The Girl in the Walls, marked the beginning of its presence in the publishing field, gaining recognition for its structural and analytical approach to understanding human influence and control.

Khambrath Group functions as both a publishing entity and a creative management platform for Fahadh MizhVer's intellectual works. The company emphasizes ownership, originality, and the direct connection between author and audience—reflecting its founder's belief in independent creation and controlled distribution.
